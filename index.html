Core Loop

WHAT THE PLAYER ACTUALLY DOES

The best publisher pitch is a loop, not a lore summary. Players explore, identify the obstacle, use the right animal ability or switch roles, trigger a satisfying or silly outcome, and push into the next challenge.

Explore compact spaces
Readable 2D spaces let players quickly spot routes, hazards, and interaction opportunities.
Swap or cooperate
Solo players alternate roles while co-op players divide tasks naturally, creating two valid play fantasies from one design spine.
Solve by personality
Each character is functionally different enough that choosing who acts becomes part of the fun.

Positioning

NOT JUST CUTE, BUT MARKETABLY DISTINCT

Reference games are useful only when they explain audience fit and product positioning. The goal is to show familiarity without sounding derivative.

Reference language
  • Rayman for readable platforming energy and approachable movement rhythm.
  • Mario for broad accessibility, toy-like joy, and instant family-friendly readability.
  • Ori for puzzle pacing and elegant environment-led progression.
Ownable angle
  • Dual-animal identity gives the game a silhouette and storefront memory hook.
  • Solo swap plus co-op lets one mechanic base support two player fantasies.
  • Clip-friendly chaos makes the game easier to surface outside traditional platformer audiences.
Story

THE STORY SUPPORTS THE PLAY

When the Pig King's minions raid a peaceful squirrel village and steal its precious acorns, Dachip and Gordia set out to take them back. The setup is simple, legible, and emotionally clean, which is exactly why it works for this kind of action-adventure pitch: it gives the player a reason to move without burying the game under lore.
